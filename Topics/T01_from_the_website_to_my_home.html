From the website to the living room, how does my favorite piece of furniture get to my home?

The days of the Internet have given us many new opportunities: for example, we can chat with friends around the world without further ado, get information quickly on all conceivable topics and even obtain an educational qualification online. It is of course particularly interesting to shop comfortably and safely from home. With the fast pace of the information superhighway, however, so does impatience in view of seemingly long delivery times. If the reasons for this are known, the waiting time will certainly be easier, which is why we would like to give you a look behind the scenes of Meble kolonialne 24 today and show you the sometimes adventurous ways your furniture comes to you.

From raw material to solid furniture
  1. It all begins in distant India, where a small sprout struggles through the earth on a plantation and grows into a stately tree, the natural material of our solid furniture. Our woods come from plantations e.g. in Rajasthan and the neighboring states of Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, Punjab, Haryana and the Gujra by the sea.

  2. The raw logs are loaded onto trucks and sometimes transported hundreds of kilometers over dusty roads and mountainous terrain to the sawmill near the respective production site, e.g. to Jodhpur, where our colonial style program Oxford is made.

  3. Here, the raw wood is cut into boards of various thicknesses and lengths and parched, i.e. dried, and off you go to production.

  4. We are now in a workshop in Jodhpur. Orders come in all the time, different furniture series and new designs are to be produced, there are reorders for series that are already in progress and everything in the process has to be coordinated and combined with one another. The paintwork is still missing here, the ordered tables are waiting for their packaging.

  5. Everything should be loaded into the container, mostly without pallets, in order to fully utilize the space down to the last corner.

  6. When everything is in place and nothing wobbles, the doors are sealed and now the journey really begins.

  7. In a well-rehearsed rhythm, the furniture loads leave the place of manufacture and find themselves in the port of Mundra, which is over 600 km away. Off on the ship and directly to the port of Hamburg will now be shipped. Before our forwarding company is allowed to receive the containers here, customs first and foremost have the pleasure. After the necessary paperwork has been properly dealt with, the next station can finally be targeted: the company meblekolonialne24 in the town of Bochnia near Krakow, Lesser Poland.

  8. With the last breath your favorite piece is now getting ready for the last part of the more than 9,000 km long journey. On such a long journey, of course, disabilities can arise over which one has little control. The monsoons or other violent weather conditions make routes difficult to access and some transport literally falls into the water.

  9. With the address label, it is assigned its destination and the transport company ensures a safe and agreed delivery. Finally arrived, all that remains is to unpack.
When the long-awaited piece of furniture is finally at your home, you will now know that it has been on a really adventurous journey. You will surely enjoy the special and high-quality furniture for a very long time.
Notice: It can happen that certain lengths of wood are currently difficult to grasp for production or that no containers are available, and ships have already been overbooked, which again leads to new waiting times. In addition, there are only 1 to 2 loading days per week in Mundra, so you have to queue at the back. Again and again there are controls and visual inspections by the authorities in India, just like in Poland, and that too takes time.
Note: Incidentally, there is no work on holy holidays such as Holy and Diwali in India, but these are at least predictable.
