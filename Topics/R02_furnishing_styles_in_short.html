Furnishing styles in short

You're wondering which style may suits your home? Here you'll find basic information about most popular of them which help you made the decision.

The search for new furniture isn't always easy and is often annoying and time-consuming. Furniture shops on the Internet or in the vicinity offer numerous designs and styles. From classic to rustic to simply modern furniture, a wide range of products is offered that makes the question of the right type of furnishing much more difficult.

In this blog post we'll introduce you to the most popular designs and explain which one suits you.

Table 1.
Style Picture Range example Description Origin For whom
Colonial OXFORD A historical architectural style that even goes back well into the 16th or 17th century and shows typical elements of the Baroque and Renaissance periods. The style of living itself emerged in the 19th century and became popular in mansions in India and the African continent during the times of colonization. This style suits those who value classic and solidity. Subtle curvings and iron elements highlight the colonial origin.
Industrial INDUSTRIAL The industrial style is a style that stands out particularly with the combination of wood and metal. In general, this furniture evokes a cool loft character and leaves a lasting impression on the viewer. Industrial interior design takes inspiration from factories built during the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Buildings built of brick, concrete, iron, and steel, designed to last for years. For individualists who want to stand out with their home furnishings, the industrial style is exactly the right choice.

Since each item is unrepeatable it's the right choice for everyone who looks for uniqueness.

To visually highlight the apartment, own creations or the painting of different pieces of furniture are essential features of the loft character. In the 1940s, people converted empty halls, storage rooms, or attics into living spaces in the USA. Artists particularly sought these lofts as an inexpensive alternative to living. It's the type of furniture for you if you have a loft apartment .
