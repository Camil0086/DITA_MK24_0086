The search for new furniture is not always easy and is often annoying and time-consuming. Furniture shops on the Internet or in the vicinity offer numerous designs and styles. From classic to rustic to simply modern furniture, a wide range of products is offered that makes the question of the right type of furnishing much more difficult.

In this blog post we will introduce you to the most popular designs and explain which one suits you.

Colonial Oxford A historical architectural style that even goes back well into the 16th or 17th century and shows typical elements of the Baroque and Renaissance periods. The style of living itself emerged in the 19th century and was first used in mansions in India and in the African continent during the times of colonization. This style suits those who walue classic and solidity. Subtle curvings and iron elements highlight the kolonial origin.
Industrial Factory The industrial style is a style that stands out particularly with the combination of wood and metal. In general, this furniture evokes a cool loft character and leaves a lasting impression on the viewer. Industrial interior design takes inspiration from factories built during the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Buildings built of brick, concrete, iron, and steel, designed to last for years. For individualists who want to stand out with their home furnishings, the industrial style is exactly the right choice.
Vintage Fable The design direction is based on what is known as the “used look”, meaning traces of use brought about on purpose, which are intended to give the appearance a certain antiquity. When it comes to furniture, the vintage style means a retro look that was particularly popular in the 1930s through to the 1970s. This furnishing style combines imaginative art with nostalgia. Since each items is unrepeteable it is the right choice for everyone who looks for uniquness.

Loft Detroit This type of setup has to as simple as possible. In order to visually highlight the apartment, own creations or the painting of different pieces of furniture are essential features of the loft character. In the 1940s, empty halls, storage rooms or attics were briefly converted into living space in the USA. These lofts were particularly sought after by artists who were looking for an inexpensive alternative to living. This type of furniture is definitely recommended for those who also have a loft apartment.
