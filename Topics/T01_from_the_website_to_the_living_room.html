From the website to the living room

How does your favorite piece of furniture get to your home? Get familiar with the particular steps of the journey.

The days of the Internet have given us many new opportunities: for example, we can chat with friends around the world without further ado, get information quickly on all possible topics and even get an educational qualification online. It's of course particularly interesting to shop comfortably and safely from home. But together with the development of information superhighway, increase the impatience in view of seemingly long delivery times. If the reasons for this are known, the waiting time will certainly be easier, which is why we would like to give you a look behind the scenes of Meble kolonialne 24 today and show you the sometimes adventurous ways your furniture comes to you.

From raw material to solid furniture
  1. It all begins in distant India, where a small sprout struggles through the earth on a plantation and grows into a majestic tree, the natural material of our solid furniture. Our woods come from plantations e.g. in Rajasthan and the neighboring states of Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, Punjab, Haryana, and the Gujra by the sea.

  2. Lumbermen load the raw logs onto trucks and then drivers sometimes transport them hundreds of kilometers over dusty roads and mountainous terrain to the sawmill near the respective production site, e.g. to Jodhpur, where craftsmen make our colonial style program OXFORD.

  3. Here, sawmill workers cut the raw wood into boards of various thicknesses and lengths. Dried planks are ready to go to production.

  4. We're now in a workshop in Jodhpur. Orders come in all the time. Different furniture series and new designs emerge, there are reorders for series that are already in progress. Vendor provides coordination of everything in the process and combination with one another. The paintwork is still missing here, the ordered tables are waiting for their packaging.

  5. Workers load everything into the container, mostly without pallets, to fully utilize the space down to the last corner.

  6. When everything is in place and nothing wobbles, our agent can seal the doors, and now the journey really begins.

  7. In a well-rehearsed rhythm, the furniture loads leave the place of manufacture and find themselves in the port of Mundra, which is over 600 km away. Now the container goes on the ship and directly to the port of Hamburg. From there, after few days his next stop is the harbor in Gdynia. Before our forwarding company will receive the containers here, customs first have the pleasure. After the necessary paperwork, the truck takes the load to our warehouse in the town of Bochnia near Krakow, Lesser Poland.

  8. With the last breath, your favorite piece is now getting ready for the last part of the more than 9,000 km long journey. On such a long journey, of course, disabilities can arise over which one has little control. The monsoons or other violent weather conditions make routes difficult to access and some transport falls into the water.

  9. With the address label and known destination, the transport company ensures a safe and agreed delivery. It finally arrived, all that remains is to unpack.
When the long-awaited piece of furniture is finally at your home, you'll now know that it has been on an adventurous journey. You'll surely enjoy the special and high-quality furniture for a very long time.
Notice: Certain lengths of wood may be currently difficult to grasp for production or that no containers are available. Ships have already been overbooked, which again leads to new waiting times. In addition, there are only 1 to 2 loading days per week in Mundra, so you've to queue at the back if you arrived too late. Again and again, there are controls and visual inspections by the authorities in India, just like in Poland, and that too takes time.
Note: Incidentally, there's no work on holidays such as Holy and Diwali in India, but these we can at least predict.
